Base electrical unit,the ampere,is the unit for electrical current.

Derived units: Electrical and magnetic units that use various combinations of base units in their definitions.

Types of Materials

Conductors

Insulators

Semi-conductors

Conductors: have free electrons that can move relatively free through the material.

Insulators: have electrons bound to atoms and cannot move freely through the material.

Semi-conductors: have properties between insulators and conductors.

Charge a Conductor by Induction

When a charged object is brought near a conductor, the electric field of the charged object causes the charges within the conductor to redistribute.

Requires no contact with the object inducing the charge.

Coulomb’s Law

The force is directly proportional to the product of the charges and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.

Electrostatic (Coulomb) force is a vector quantity.

Multiple Charges

If more than two charges are present, the resultant force on any one charge equals the vector sum of the forces exerted by the other individual charges that are present.
